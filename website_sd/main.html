Context & Objective

We are collaborating with our training partner, which is the Percussive Arts Society. The focus of our project is more centered on the pedagogic area, in specific, for the beginners on percussion arts, so they can get familiarized with different rhythms, percussive instruments sounds, tempos, etc.

So the General idea of the game is for the begginer percussive student, to familiarize, associate and to recognize sound for reply to stimuly, and meassuring tempos, for completing a level and letting the user get better and faster.

Basically, the game is composed of a progression of attack and defense rounds. Each round starts with an attack phase, where the player can see some aliens getting into the scene. The aliens will appear on the map with a specific rhythm and with some percussion audio, depending on the type of alien, that the player must memorize for the second phase of the round. After the attack aliens are presented to the player, the game will zoom-int into the player’s ship, so he can no longer see the incoming aliens, but the player will be forced to memorize the rhythm and must repeat the rhythm to defend himself. The player will complete a level if he successfully destroys the aliens without losing all his life. After the user completes all the rounds, he will have the option to continue, but each time, the rhythm will get faster and faster. The aliens will descend from four invisible columns, and so the player will have four buttons for shooting ramrods to destroy the aliens.

The four default controls of the game will be set to be ‘a’, ‘s’, ‘d’, and ‘f’. The player will be able to change the control layout to be whatever keyboard keys to be, on the configuration menu.
